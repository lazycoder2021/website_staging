M365 Updates Jan 01, 2045

Unveiling New Frontiers: Microsoft 365 Copilot's April Enhancements

We understand that time is luxury most can’t afford and reading through laborious text is another effort. So, our aim is to give you crisp, reliable, and meaningful updates in easily readable form.

  • Starting 2nd April, 2024, Copilot is available to all M365 license holder.
  • Daily chat limits on Copilot queries eradicated.
  • From May, 2024, users can make 100 image gen requests in MS Designer.
  • Commercial Data Protection for Copilot web chats.
  • MS will NOT retain even your prompts or chats; won’t use them to train LLMs.

Microsoft's latest announcement on April 2, 2024, brings a wave of innovation and efficiency to Microsoft 365 (M365) users. Most importantly it removes the fears about AI and data usage. Here's a succinct breakdown of what this means for you:

Empowering Work with AI: All M365 commercial license holders now enjoy unlimited access to GPT-4 Turbo via Copilot for Microsoft 365. This enhancement eradicates daily chat limitations, allowing users and teams to leverage Copilot for an infinite number of queries, from scheduling emails to generating SharePoint content.

Boundless Queries, Boundless Potential: Both Copilot for M365 and Copilot Web users can now query without limits. This opens a realm of possibilities for enhancing productivity and creativity within the workspace.

Privacy, A Top Priority: Microsoft reassures users of the stringent privacy measures in place. Whether it's Copilot for web or M365, your data remains strictly yours. Microsoft's commitment to privacy ensures that prompts, responses, or any chat data are not retained or used to train AI models, offering peace of mind to all users.

Click here to know more about Microsoft’s Commercial Data Protection.

A Boost for Creativity: In another exciting development, Microsoft Designer is set to receive an upgrade. Starting May 2024, users will be able to make up to 100 rapid image generation requests daily, a significant increase from the current limit of 15.

This comprehensive update from Microsoft not only enhances the Copilot experience but also reaffirms the company's dedication to user privacy and data security. For a deeper dive into these updates and their implications, visiting the Microsoft 365 blog is highly recommended.
